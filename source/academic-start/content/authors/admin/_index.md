Subrata Paul is a Bioinformatics Scientist at the Integrated Data Sciences Section (IDSS) within the National Institutes of Health (NIH). With a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics from the University of Colorado Denver, Dr. Paul bridges the gap between sophisticated mathematical modeling and complex biological data.

His work focuses on statistical genetics and multi-omics integration, where he applies advanced computational strategies to understand the genetic architecture of human diseases. His research spans a diverse range of critical health areas, including autoimmune disorders (such as SLE and vitiligo), infectious diseases, and telomere biology disorders. Dr. Paul specializes in developing and applying novel statistical methods--including mixture models and survival analysis techniques--to uncover hidden heterogeneity in large-scale genomic and single-cell RNA-sequencing datasets.
